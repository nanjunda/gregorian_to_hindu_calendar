Understanding the GPS of the Ancients

1. The Cosmic Race Track

Imagine the Solar System as a giant race track. The Sun is in the center, and the planets are cars racing around it.

The Zodiac isn't magic—it's just the "Stadium Seating." It’s the band of stars surrounding the track. When we say "Mars is in Aries," it just means: "If you look at the Mars car from Earth, the Aries section of the stadium is behind it."

2. The Great Wobble

Have you ever spun a toy top? As it slows down, it starts to wobble. Earth does the same thing.

This wobble is super slow—one circle takes 26,000 years! Because of this, the "West" (Tropical Zodiac) and the "East" (Vedic/Sidereal Zodiac) have drifted apart.

Western Astrology freezes the sky as it was 2,000 years ago. Vedic Astronomy tracks where the stars actually are right now.

3. The Mystery of Nodes

You will see points called Rahu and Ketu. In stories, they are dragons.

In science, they are Intersection Points. Think of two Hula Hoops: the Gold one is Earth’s path, the Blue one is the Moon’s.

The Blue hoop is tilted. Rahu and Ketu are the only two spots where the hoops cross. Eclipses can only happen at these exact danger zones!
