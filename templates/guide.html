3. The Mystery of the "Shadow Planets"

In the app, you will see two mysterious points called Rahu and Ketu. In mythology, they are demons who swallow the Sun and Moon. But astronomically, they are something even cooler: The Lunar Nodes.

Imagine two hula hoops:
1. Gold Hoop: The Earth's path around the Sun.
2. Silver Hoop: The Moon's path around the Earth.

The Silver hoop is tilted by about 5 degrees. It doesn't lie flat. The two points where the hoops cross each other are Rahu (North Node) and Ketu (South Node).

Fig 3: Rahu and Ketu are simply the crash zones (intersections) of the two orbits.

From Demons to Digital Satellites

So, Rahu and Ketu aren't physical rocks; they are mathematical danger zones for eclipses. An eclipse only happens when the Sun and Moon meet exactly at these crossings.

Fun Fact: It's not just ancient history. Modern satellites use these exact same "nodal points" to calculate stable orbits for GPS and communications! Ancient mythology and Modern Engineering are actually talking about the same math.
